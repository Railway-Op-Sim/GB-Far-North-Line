# Far North Line

## Route

The Far North line is a single track railway with several passing loops, running from Inverness in the south, to Wick on the north east coast of Scotland. The penultimate station, Georgemas Junction, is the northernmost railway junction in the UK, where the line to Thurso branches off. Thurso itself is the most northerly station in the UK, on the north coast of Scotland.

At Dingwall station the Kyle of Lochalsh Line branches off towards the west coast.

None of the line is electrified, and all passenger services are provided by Scotrail using Class 158 DMUs.

Several stations on the route are request stops, although this cannot be accurately represented in RailOS currently.

## Simulation

A timetable representing Wednesday 4th March has been included, with all Scotrail passenger services. Also included is a charter service from the Kyle of Lochalsh line in each direction for a bit of variety, although this didn't operate in real life.

Services on the line are very infrequent, with just 4 trains per day running the full length, although there are also a few shorter runs.

## Operational Tips

- Platforms are provided in the service descriptions where necessary for terminating trains - these should be kept to for successful operation.
- Keep an eye on arrival and departure times at passing loops to make sure trains pass in the correct places. Getting this wrong can cause lengthy delays!
- Be careful with services at Georgemas Junction. Heading north, trains reverse at Georgemas Junction and head to Thurso first, before reversing again and travelling to Wick. Southbound, the opposite occurs.
